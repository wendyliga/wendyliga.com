The Winter Solstice festival was coming soon, and Xiaoming decided to make some special red bean paste tangyuan. He invited Lili to join him in learning how to make this festive dessert. "Let's prepare the ingredients first!" Xiaoming said to Lili.

They started by measuring the red beans, rinsing them clean, and putting them in a pot to cook. While cooking the red beans, Xiaoming explained, "Cooking red beans takes time. This way, the filling of the tangyuan will be both sweet and fragrant." Lili listened carefully and nodded to show she understood.

Once the red bean paste was ready, Lili and Xiaoming prepared the tangyuan dough. Xiaoming demonstrated how to knead the glutinous rice flour, and Lili followed his example. Soon, the table was filled with round tangyuan.

"Now let's stuff the red bean paste inside!" Xiaoming said happily. Lili carefully picked a piece of tangyuan dough, placed the red bean paste in the middle, and slowly wrapped it up. "You wrap them so beautifully!" Xiaoming praised.

Finally, the tangyuan were cooked. They tasted one, and it was sweet and chewy—delicious. "Eating tangyuan during the Winter Solstice symbolizes family reunion," Xiaoming said with a smile. Lili smiled too, "This makes people feel so warm."

In this way, Xiaoming and Lili not only learned to make tangyuan, but also experienced the happiness of love and family togetherness.

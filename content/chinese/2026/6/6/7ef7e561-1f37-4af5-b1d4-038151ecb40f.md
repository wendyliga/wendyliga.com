## Grammatical Points and Analysis

### Verb-Object Phrases:
- **玩 (wán)** - to play
- **听到 (tīngdào)** - to hear (literally 'listen-arrive')
- **发抖 (fādǒu)** - to tremble (literally 'emit-tremble')
- **帮助 (bāngzhù)** - to help
- **吃东西 (chī dōngxi)** - to eat things/eat food
- **拍翅膀 (pāi chìbǎng)** - to flap wings
- **唱歌 (chànggē)** - to sing songs

### Resultative Complements:
A key grammatical point. It indicates the result of an action.
- **听到 (tīngdào):** The verb 听 (tīng - to listen) combined with 到 (dào - to arrive) means "to hear" (listening resulted in arriving at the sound).
- **找到 (zhǎodào):** Used implicitly in discovering the bird, meaning "to succeed in finding".
- **包起来 (bāo qǐlái):** The verb 包 (bāo - to wrap) followed by 起来 (qǐlái - up/rising) means "to wrap up". It indicates the action's direction and completion.
- **飞起来 (fēi qǐlái):** Similar to above, means "to fly up/take off".
- **打开 (dǎkāi):** The verb 打 (dǎ - to hit/open) with 开 (kāi - open) means "to open up completely".

### Special Sentence Structures:
- **用...把...包起来 (yòng...bǎ...bāo qǐlái):** This uses the "把 (bǎ)" sentence structure, which is very common in Chinese. It emphasizes how an object is dealt with or disposed of. `用` introduces the tool (handkerchief), and `把` introduces the object being handled (the bird).
- **往...飞 (wǎng...fēi):** This structure uses the preposition 往 (wǎng) to indicate direction, meaning "to fly toward...".

### Modal Verbs:
- **应该 (yīnggāi):** Expresses obligation, "should". ("我们应该帮助它。")
- **要 (yào):** Expresses necessity or want. ("我们要小心一点。")
- **会 (huì):** Expresses future possibility or learned skill. ("它会想家吗？" - "Will it miss home?")

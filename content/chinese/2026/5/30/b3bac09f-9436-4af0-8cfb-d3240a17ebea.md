Lili took out a piece of red colored paper and started folding. She folded very fast and very well. Xiaoming watched Lili and followed her to learn. He first folded the dragon boat's head, then the body, and finally the tail. But Xiaoming was a bit slow, and he made two mistakes. He asked, "Lili, how should this part be folded?" Lili said, "Look, fold this corner to this side." She taught him slowly.

Xiaoming finally finished folding his own dragon boat. He looked at Lili's dragon boat and then at his own. Lili's dragon boat was very beautiful; his dragon boat was a bit crooked. Xiaoming said, "You folded really well! I lost." Lili smiled and said, "It's okay, this is your first time folding, you already did very well. We can put the dragon boats in the water together."

They decided to wait until the Dragon Boat Festival day and put the two dragon boats in the water for a race. Xiaoming was very happy, and he said, "In one month, we'll have another competition. I will definitely fold better!"

## Grammatical Points and Analysis

### Resultative Complements (结果补语)
- **折错了** (zhé cuò le) — "folded wrong". The verb 折 (fold) is followed by the complement 错 (wrong) to indicate the result of the action.
- **折好了** (zhé hǎo le) — "finished folding". The complement 好 (done/finished) shows the action was completed successfully.

### 把 (bǎ) Structure
- **把这个角折到这边** (bǎ zhè ge jiǎo zhé dào zhè biān) — "fold this corner to this side". The 把 structure emphasizes the object (the corner) and the action performed on it.

### 了 (le) for Completed Action or Change of State
- **一个月后就是端午节了** — The 了 at the end indicates a new situation (it will be the festival).
- **折好了** — The 了 marks the completion of folding.

### 得 (de) Degree Complement
- **折得又快又好** (zhé de yòu kuài yòu hǎo) — "folded fast and well". 得 connects the verb to descriptive phrases.
- **折得真好啊** (zhé de zhēn hǎo a) — "folded really well".

### 跟 (gēn) Meaning "to Follow"
- **跟着她学** (gēnzhe tā xué) — "follow her to learn". 跟 means "with" or "to follow".

### 又...又... (yòu...yòu...) Structure
- **又快又好** (yòu kuài yòu hǎo) — "both fast and good". Used to combine two positive qualities.

### Verb Reduplication (动词重叠) for Casual Action
- **看看** (kànkan) — "take a look". Reduplication makes the action sound more casual or brief.
